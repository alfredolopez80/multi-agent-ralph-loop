# Banned Phrases

## Throat-Clearing Openers

Generic openings that delay the actual content:

- "Certainly!"
- "It is important to note that..."
- "It should be noted that..."
- "It is worth noting that..."
- "It is crucial to understand that..."
- "It is essential to..."
- "It is interesting to note that..."
- "It is often said that..."
- "As we all know..."
- "As you may know..."
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..."
- "In this day and age..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Let's dive right in..."
- "Without further ado..."
- "Here's the thing:"
- "Let that sink in."
- "This matters because..."

## Emphasis Crutches

Overused emphasis patterns that weaken writing:

- "plays a crucial role"
- "plays a vital role"
- "plays a key role"
- "is absolutely essential"
- "is particularly important"
- "cannot be overstated"
- "is worth its weight in gold"
- "is a game-changer"
- "is groundbreaking"
- "is revolutionary"
- "is transformative"
- "takes center stage"
- "is the cornerstone of..."
- "is the backbone of..."
- "is the heart and soul of..."
- "is paramount"
- "is of utmost importance"

- "circle back to"
- "deep dive"
- "synergy"
- "leverage"
- "optimize"
- "streamline"
- "pivot"
- "granular"
- "touch base"
- "bandwidth"
- "low-hanging fruit"
- "move the needle"
- "think outside the box"
- "drill down"
- "on the same page"
- "get ahead of the curve"
- "navigate" (for challenges)
- "unpack" (before analysis)

- "in the event that"
- "at this point in time"
- "in regard to"
- "with respect to"
- "pertaining to"
- "in the process of"
- "with regard to"
- "for the purpose of"
- "in order to"
- "in lieu of"

## Meta-Commentary

Unnecessary commentary about the writing:

- "As mentioned above..."
- "As previously stated..."
- "As noted earlier..."
- "As we have seen..."
- "As we saw earlier..."
- "This demonstrates that..."
- "This illustrates that..."
- "This shows that..."
- "This proves that..."
- "This highlights the importance of..."
- "This underscores the significance of..."
- "It goes without saying that..."
- "Needless to say..."
- "By and large..."
- "All things considered..."
